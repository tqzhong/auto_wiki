# Memex (Vannevar Bush, 1945)

## Overview

The **Memex** was a hypothetical device proposed by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay *"As We May Think"*. It described a personal, mechanized library that would allow an individual to store all their books, records, and communications, and to create **associative trails** — linked paths through the stored knowledge.

## Relevance to LLM Wiki

Karpathy explicitly draws the connection:

> "The idea is related in spirit to Vannevar Bush's Memex (1945) — a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents. Bush's vision was closer to this than to what the web became: private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves."

Key parallels:
- **Personal**: Both are private, individual knowledge stores (not public wikis)
- **Curated**: The owner decides what goes in (not algorithmic feeds)
- **Associative trails**: Links between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves
- **Persistent**: Knowledge accumulates over time

## What Bush Couldn't Solve

> "The part he couldn't solve was who does the maintenance."

The LLM solves this. The human curates (as Bush imagined), but the LLM handles all the maintenance — creating links, updating cross-references, keeping summaries current.
